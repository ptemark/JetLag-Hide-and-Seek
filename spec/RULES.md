# Jet Lag: Hide + Seek — Rulebook (Structured Summary)

## Overview

Hide + Seek is a real‑world transit hide‑and‑seek game.

Players:
- One **Hider**
- One team of **Seekers**

Goal:
- Hider travels to a hiding spot within a defined **map**, using transit and/or walking.
- Seekers ask questions to deduce Hider’s location.
- Hider wins by lasting longest; Seekers win by finding the Hider fastest.

---

## Game Scales

### 1) Small
- City or town area
- 30–60 min hiding period
- Hiding zone: 500 m from transit station

### 2) Medium
- Larger city or metro
- ~60–180 min
- Hiding zone: 500 m

### 3) Large
- Entire region or country
- 180 min or more
- Hiding zone: 1 km around station

*Choose scale before playing.* :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

---

## Setup

### Map
- Define borders
- Include transit systems: trains, buses, ferries, trams
- Must have sufficient stations (100+ recommended)

### Players
- Hider gets a **Hider Deck**
- Seekers get the **Investigation Book**
- Dice and maps recommended

### Starting Position
- All players begin at a common starting point
- Hider selects hiding time period

---

## Hiding Rules

1. Hider moves using public transit and/or walking.
2. Hider’s **hiding zone** is centered on the final transit station reached.
3. At end of hiding period, Hider must remain within their hiding zone.
4. Hider cannot move during **End Game**. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

---

## Asking Questions

Seekers may ask questions to locate the Hider.

### Question Timing
- Ask a question only after the previous question is answered.
- Hider must answer truthfully within time limits:
    - Standard: **5 min**
    - Photo: **10–20 min (varies by game size)**

### Categories (Examples)
1. **Matching Questions**
    - Compare nearest features (e.g., nearest airport match?)
2. **Measuring Questions**
    - Are they closer/further to something than Seekers?
3. **Transit Questions**
    - E.g., is hider’s station on your current transit route?
4. **Photo Questions**
    - Requires Hider to send a photo matching criteria
5. **Thermometer Questions**
    - Based on movement relative to Seekers (colder/warmer)
6. **Tentacle Questions**
    - Ask about nearby locations (e.g., within X km)

*Each category has card costs and effects.* :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

---

## Cards (Hider Deck)

Types:
- **Time bonus cards**
- **Powerup cards**
- **Curse cards**

Rules:
- Hider may hold up to 6 cards
- Drawing depends on question answered
- Cards can affect timing and movement

---

## End Game

- Begins when Seekers enter Hider’s hiding zone *and* are off transit
- Hider must stay put in a public hiding spot
- Seekers must **spot** Hider (within ~2 m) to finalize capture
- Time bonuses from cards are added to Hider’s total if captured

---

## Winning Conditions

- **Longest hiding time** (highest net score) wins
- If Hider is not found within period, they get maximum score
- Seekers aim to minimize time until capture

---

## Optional Variants

### Two Teams of Seekers
- Play as separate seeker teams
- Optional competitive format

### Experimental Rules
- Custom map shapes
- Custom transit definitions

---

## Miscellaneous Rules

### Safety
- Exclude unsafe areas from maps
- Players agree on boundaries beforehand

### Mapping Apps
- Recommend using reliable apps like Google Maps
- Distance measuring and icons matter for question accuracy

### GPS Tracking
- Optional but recommended
- Track Seekers and Hider during runs

---

## Definitions

**Hiding Zone**
- A radius around the station
    - Small/Medium: 500 m
    - Large: 1 km

**End Game**
- Seekers off transit + inside hiding zone

**Transit Station**
- Location where public transit stops
